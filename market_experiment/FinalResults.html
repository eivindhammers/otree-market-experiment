What did we learn?

This experiment demonstrates a fundamental insight in economics: markets can aggregate dispersed private information.

  • Each participant only knew their own value or cost
  • No one knew the full supply and demand curves
  • Yet the market price converged toward the competitive equilibrium
  • Trades occurred between high-value buyers and low-cost sellers
  • This created economic surplus (gains from trade)

This is the "invisible hand" at work: decentralized decisions by self-interested individuals can lead to socially efficient outcomes, without any central planner knowing everyone's preferences.

Key takeaway: Prices serve as signals that coordinate economic activity, even when information is dispersed across many individuals.
